Newton Fund Researcher Links provides opportunities for early career researchers from the UK, Brazil and Uruguay to interact and explore opportunities for building long-lasting research collaborations.

Uruguay and Brazil share common challenges in agriculture and food production, from livestock farming and selective breeding to biofuel roduction, which form a significant part of both economies. Bio-soft matter science (experiments and theory) can directly tackle these. This is an explorative workshop focused on building up new collaborations.

No experience in agrifood production research is needed, or if you work in agrifood area, on soft matter science. The workshop will focus n soft-matter science with the novel view point of agrifood challenges. This encompasses areas such Elastography, mechanical properties of oft material, mathematical biology and biophysics, fluid mechanics, solid mechanics and elasticity, stochastic and dynamical systems in a ide ange of length-scales, from single molecule to population dynamics.

Other specific areas: Animal reproduction, livestock production, plant and animal science, including aquaculture systems, biofuel roduction and biofilm formation. Meat/fish production and meat elastography. Water science, water filtration, waterborn infections, bacterial rowth and antibiotic bacterial-resistance and viruses, elasticity related diseases either at cell or macroscopic level. Participants are encouraged to bring other cross-related areas of interest.
