Mathematical modelling of human sperm swimming

The current "Computer-Aided Semen Analysis" (CASA) system used in clinics is a poor predictor of fertility as it only measures head movement, ignoring the flagellum, which s n important, subtle predictor of fertility and sperm health. Thus, semen samples are assessed with little information about the sperm

Objectives

To develop a mathematical tool linking the sperm head motion to that of the flagella. This will allow in-depth analysis of the sperm’s nternal mechanics, providing a practical indicator of sperm viability. This will be combined with cheap technologies to recognize wimming atterns of the most “healthy” sperm.

Novelty

We will use mathematical modelling simulations to understand the link between flagellar beat and swimming motion, introducing entirely ew fertility parameters (e.g. sperm energy efficiency).

Experimental and theoretical approach

We will feed high spatiotemporal microscope images of the flagellum into a virtual mathematical sperm model to perform simulations of e swimming sperm. From these flagellar movements, swimming pattern can be predicted.
